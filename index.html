Great Wall of China

An architectural marvel that spans over 21,000 kilometers, originally built by Emperor Qin Shi Huang and expanded by the Ming Dynasty.

Petra, Jordan

Often called the 'Lost City' because it remained unknown to the Western world until 1812, it is now famous for its Treasury building which appeared in the movie Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade.

The Colosseum, Italy

"Located in the center of Rome, this oval amphitheater could hold up to 80,000 spectators. It was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and dramas based on Classical mythology.

Chichen Itza, Mexico

"Located in the center of Rome, this oval amphitheater could hold up to 80,000 spectators. It was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and dramas based on Classical mythology.

Machu Picchu, Peru

Built in the 15th century and later abandoned, this site sits on a mountain ridge 2,430 meters above sea level. It is renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without mortar.

Taj Mahal, India

A masterpiece of Mughal architecture, it includes a mosque, a guest house, and formal gardens. The white marble changes color depending on the time of day-pinkish in the morning, milky white in the evening, and golden at night.

Christ the Redeemer, Brazil

Standing 30 meters tall, its open arms symbolize peace and welcome. It overlooks the entire city of Rio and is considered a cultural icon of both Brazil and Christianity.

Great Pyramid of Giza

Built as a tomb for the Pharaoh Khufu, it stood as the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years. It consists of an estimated 2.3 million blocks of stone.
